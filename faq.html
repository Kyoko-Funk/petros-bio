Common Questions

Get answers to the questions our clients ask most frequently.

What is Pétros Biokinetics?

Pétros Biokinetics is a clinical restoration methodology combining biomechanical assessment, movement prescriptions, and supervised execution protocols. We specialize in addressing chronic pain, movement dysfunction, athletic optimization, and post-injury rehabilitation through evidence-based restoration.

How long does the restoration process typically take?

Timeline depends on your condition, goals, and commitment level. Most clients see significant improvements in 6-12 weeks with consistent protocols. Chronic conditions may require 12-16 weeks for complete restoration. During your initial assessment, we'll provide a personalized timeline based on your biomechanical profile.

Do I need medical clearance before starting?

For clients with acute injuries, recent surgery, or serious health conditions, we recommend medical clearance. However, for chronic pain, postural issues, and preventive optimization, medical clearance is not required. During your consultation, we'll assess whether any medical sign-off is needed for your specific situation.

What's the difference between your method and physical therapy?

While physical therapy addresses injury recovery, our method focuses on complete biomechanical restoration and optimization. We assess your entire movement system, prescribe targeted protocols, and supervise execution. This is more comprehensive and goal-oriented, addressing not just pain relief but complete functional restoration and performance enhancement.

Can I do the protocols at home, or do I need to visit your facility?

While some maintenance protocols can be done at home, we strongly recommend in-facility execution initially. Our supervised sessions ensure proper form, progression, and adjustments based on your real-time biomechanical feedback. Once you've mastered the movement patterns (typically 6-8 weeks), you can transition to home maintenance with periodic check-ins.

Which medical aid schemes do you accept?

We accept most major medical aid schemes including:

  • • NHP
  • • NMC
  • • NAMMED
  • • RMA
  • • Prosperity

Note: We do NOT accept Heritage.

For PSEMAS patients: We issue an invoice for your session. Payment is required upfront, and you can submit the claim to PSEMAS for direct reimbursement.

What should I expect during my first assessment?

Your initial assessment (60 minutes) includes a comprehensive movement analysis, biomechanical evaluation, discussion of your health history and goals, functional tests, and a detailed explanation of findings. You'll receive a customized restoration protocol with clear timelines and progress markers. No treatment occurs during this visit—it's purely diagnostic and planning.

How frequently should I schedule sessions?

Most clients begin with 2-3 sessions weekly (8 weeks), then transition to 1 session weekly for 4-8 weeks, and finally maintenance sessions monthly. Frequency depends on your condition severity, recovery pace, and goals. We adjust your schedule based on progress during your initial consultation and adjust as needed.

Is Pétros suitable for athletes and fitness enthusiasts?

Absolutely. Our Athletic Optimization service is specifically designed for athletes and fitness professionals seeking competitive advantage, injury prevention, and performance enhancement. We analyze your sport-specific movement patterns and create protocols that amplify power, efficiency, and resilience while preventing injury.

What if I don't see results after my restoration protocol?

We guarantee visible improvement within the first month with consistent protocol adherence. If you're not seeing results, we conduct a reassessment to identify barriers—whether biomechanical, lifestyle-related, or underlying conditions requiring medical investigation. We adjust protocols accordingly or refer you to specialists if needed. Your success is our priority.

What should I wear to my appointment?

Wear comfortable, loose-fitting clothing that allows easy movement, such as tracksuit pants, a t-shirt, and trainers. Avoid jeans or restrictive clothing that might limit your range of motion during assessment and treatment sessions.

What should I bring to my first visit?

Please bring:

  • • Your medical aid card
  • • Medical reports or referral letters (if available)
  • • Your ID or Passport
  • • A water bottle
What is your cancellation policy?

We require 24 hours notice for cancellations to allow us to offer the slot to another patient. Late cancellations may be subject to a cancellation fee. Your time is valuable, as is ours, and we appreciate your consideration.

Still have questions?

We're here to answer any additional questions you have about our methodology, services, or what to expect.
